Sam Wane (MEng) - Robotics Engineer – CV

Date of birth:09/05/1970

Personal Profile

I have an electronic engineering background which has developed from a hobby, my main interest is in robotics and this is where my expertise in interfacing and control comes from, I also enjoy computer programming. I love my work as a lecturer as it involves working with people and ideas whilst giving me scope to persue research interests. I have spent several years in industry, mainly in R&D, and have designed and completed several large projects in industry and education.

Relevant Work Experience

Staffordshire University, Stafford (May 2000-Current (May 2004)) - Senior Lecturer in Simulation and Virtual Reality, Control Systems and Robotics. Click here for some of my courses.

Carlisle College, Carlisle (March 1999 - March 2000) - Robotics Lecturer (part-time). This was as an evening lecturer to part-time HNC Mechanical Engineers and was achieved alongside working at McVities. My skills acquired in my full time job were shared with the students.

McVitie's, Carlisle (April 1998 - March 2000) - Robotics Engineer. Initially, directing a project to build a Robot Development Laboratory, and specifying the robots and hardware to develop robotics for use in the biscuit factory. The RDL has been used to develop gripper systems, sensors, vision and programming techniques to handle the delicate and varied biscuits within the factory. Projects included the packing of biscuits using a Scara robot.

Celestica, Kidsgrove, Staffordshire (September 1997-April 1998) - Embedded Programmer
I was responsible for developing the software within an extremely successful anti-theft vehicle location device utilising GPS and sending its location using SMS. I also modified the address book software on a Palm handheld device so it could communicate with the system inside the vehicle.

Rehab Robotics, Staffordshire University (September 1996-September 1997) - Graduate Robotics Researcher. I designed and wrote the controlling software for the embedded PC within the robot 'Handy-1'. This is a very successful rehabilitation robot on the market that allows people with no arms, or lost their use to feed, wash and shave themselves. This involved solving inverse and forward kinematics for direct control of the joint motors, Jacobian matrix and speech synthesis.

Self Employed (1990-1992) - Computer Programmer. The writing of a variety of programs especially made to companies' requests. The programs were written in Pascal and C. Other projects included the development of double glazing software, and tutoring companies' personnel in software.
